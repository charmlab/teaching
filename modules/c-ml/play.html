Nearest Neighbours. Given a query point x, measure its distance to every training point, keep the k closest, and predict by majority vote. The distance metric determines the shape of the neighbourhood: L¹ gives diamond-shaped balls, L² gives circles, L∞ gives squares. Try switching metrics while watching the neighbourhood region change — the boundary between classes shifts. Lec 02 — Nearest Neighbours & Distance Metrics

Bias-Variance Tradeoff. Imagine training the same model 50 times on 50 different (bootstrap) samples of data. Each run produces a slightly different prediction for the same test input. Variance is how much those predictions spread. Bias is how far the average prediction sits from the true answer. Small k memorises noise (high variance), large k oversmoothes (high bias). The optimal k balances both. Lec 07 — Bias-Variance Decomposition

The ℓp Unit Ball. The ℓp norm of a vector x is ‖x‖p = (|x₁|p + |x₂|p)1/p. The unit ball — the set of all points at distance ≤ 1 from the origin — changes shape as p varies. p = 1 gives a diamond, p = 2 a circle, p → ∞ a square. The choice of p in kNN changes which points are considered “near”, and changes the shape of the decision neighbourhood. Lec 02 — Distance Metrics & ℓp Norms
